# The Longing

I stand upon the shore and watch the waves unravel time, each crest a memory, each trough a thought I left behind, and the seagulls cry their plaintive songs across the salted air while I wonder if the ocean knows the weight I try to bear as the tide retreats, then rushes back with such persistent grace, obscuring yesterday's fresh tracks upon this sandy place, and the sun-dappled horizon paints promises of lands unseen while I remain anchored to this present tense, dwelling on what has been, as the city sleeps beneath a veil of muted, silver light, with distant windows flickering like the fireflies of night, and a train's low whistle mourns the miles it must forever span, connecting towns and souls and dreams as best a railroad can, while the coffee shop stays open for the lonely and the lost, where conversations overheard become a midnight's cost, and the jukebox plays a melody from some forgotten year while strangers share their loneliness without a trace of fear, and in autumn woods where amber leaves confess their final breath, the trees stand tall in naked truth, unafraid of death, while squirrels gather acorns with a frantic sort of cheer, preparing for the winter's grasp they know is drawing near, and a solitary mushroom sprouts from mossy, ancient stone, content to live its brief existence quietly alone, while the stream that once ran boisterous now whispers soft and slow as if it knows some sacred truth the rushing cannot know, so let us walk these winding paths where endings meet the start and find the courage to embrace the art within the heart because every season has its song, each loss contains its gain, and joy and sorrow intertwine like sunshine mixed with rain, and the poem of a lifetime isn't written in one breath but gathered piece by piece from life and love and death, so stand here with me, hand in hand, beneath the aging moon, and help me write the final verse that we will finish soon.
